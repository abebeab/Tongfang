The FW2722 Two-Stage Station is an advanced manual fire alarm call point designed for facilities requiring controlled emergency response. Unlike conventional single-stage devices, this station provides sequential activation capabilities - first initiating an alert stage for investigation and controlled response, followed by a full alarm stage for complete evacuation when necessary. Ideal for hospitals, airports, industrial facilities, and other complex environments where staged emergency response enhances safety and operational continuity.

Product Overview

The FW2722 Two-Stage Station represents an advanced approach to manual fire alarm initiation, designed for facilities where controlled emergency response is critical. By providing sequential activation capabilities, this device allows for a measured response to potential emergencies - first notifying designated personnel for investigation while avoiding immediate full-building evacuation, then escalating to complete alarm if the situation requires it. This staged approach minimizes unnecessary evacuations while maintaining complete life safety protection.

UL 38 Staged Activation

Certified to UL Standard 38 for Manual Fire Alarm Boxes with specific provisions for staged activation systems, ensuring reliable performance and code compliance.

Controlled Response

Allows for investigation and verification before full evacuation, reducing unnecessary disruptions while maintaining complete life safety protection for true emergencies.

Operational Continuity

Minimizes disruption to critical operations in healthcare, industrial, and institutional facilities by providing a measured response to potential emergencies.

Staged Activation Advantage: Two-stage stations provide significant benefits in facilities where full evacuation causes major disruption or risk. In hospitals, they allow staff to investigate potential emergencies before evacuating vulnerable patients. In industrial facilities, they permit controlled shutdown of processes. In airports, they enable verification before evacuating terminals. This controlled response reduces false alarm evacuations by up to 70% while maintaining complete safety for genuine emergencies through automatic escalation and manual override capabilities.

Installation & Configuration

Installation Guidelines
  • Install per NFPA 72 and local code requirements
  • Mount 48" above finished floor (ADA recommendation)
  • Place in designated emergency response areas
  • Ensure clear visibility of stage indicators
  • Provide emergency response instructions nearby
Wiring Requirements
Electrical Requirements

24VDC system voltage, dual normally open contacts.

Wiring Configuration

Four-wire connection for stage separation.

Terminal Connections

Screw terminals accept 12-22 AWG wire. Clearly marked stage terminals.

Installation Steps
  1. Turn off power to fire alarm system
  2. Select mounting location per emergency plan
  3. Configure stage timing using DIP switches
  4. Mark and drill mounting holes
  5. Route wiring to installation location
  6. Connect wires to appropriate stage terminals
  7. Mount station to wall using provided hardware
  8. Test both stage operations individually
  9. Verify proper notification at control panel
  10. Train designated personnel on operation

Product Overview

The FW151-FP Fire Phone is a dedicated emergency communication device designed for fire alarm and life safety systems. It provides reliable two-way voice communication between building occupants and emergency responders, allowing for clear instructions, status updates, and assistance coordination during emergencies.

Two-Way Communication

Clear two-way voice communication with noise-canceling microphone and weather-resistant speaker.

Emergency Call Initiation

Push-button or handset lift call initiation with visual and audible indicators.

Weather Resistant

IP54 rating for reliable operation in indoor and outdoor environments.

Key Characteristics

Clear Audio: 2W RMS output with >60dB SNR

Noise Canceling: Electret microphone for clear voice pickup

Easy Operation: Push-button or handset lift call initiation

Reliable: IP54 weatherproof construction

Durable: Polycarbonate enclosure (UL94 V-0)

Simple Wiring: 2-wire audio circuit installation

Emergency Communication Importance: During emergencies, clear communication can be life-saving. Fire phones provide a dedicated, reliable communication channel that remains operational when regular phone systems may fail. They allow emergency responders to gather critical information and provide specific instructions to building occupants.

Placement Considerations: Fire phones should be installed in locations where they are easily accessible during emergencies but protected from damage. Consider areas near exits, in stairwells, elevator lobbies, and other locations specified by building codes and emergency response plans.

Installation Guidelines
  • Install 48-60" (1.2-1.5m) above finished floor
  • Follow NEC and NFPA 72 requirements
  • Ensure clear visibility and accessibility
  • Provide adequate lighting for operation
  • Avoid locations with excessive ambient noise
  • Use proper conduit and wiring methods
  • Ensure secure mounting to wall surface

Installation Steps
  1. Select installation location per NFPA 72 requirements
  2. Mount phone securely to wall at proper height
  3. Route power and audio wiring in approved conduit
  4. Connect 24VDC power with proper polarity
  5. Connect 2-wire audio circuit to terminals
  6. Test call initiation and indicator functions
  7. Verify two-way audio communication clarity
  8. Test with fire alarm control panel interface
  9. Verify operation during alarm conditions
  10. Document installation and testing results
  11. Provide user instructions and signage

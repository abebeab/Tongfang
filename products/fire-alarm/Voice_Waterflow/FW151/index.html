Product Overview

The FW151 Voice Control is an advanced digital voice evacuation system designed for modern fire alarm and emergency notification applications. Using high-quality digital audio processing technology, it delivers clear, intelligible voice messages that provide specific instructions during emergencies, greatly enhancing occupant safety and evacuation efficiency.

Clear Voice Messages

High-quality digital audio with >70dB SNR for clear, intelligible voice instructions.

Multi-Language Support

Supports up to 8 languages for diverse occupant requirements.

Zone Control

Independent control of up to 32 zones for targeted messaging.

Key Characteristics

Clear Audio: 25W RMS per channel with >70dB SNR

Message Storage: Up to 64 pre-recorded messages

Multi-Language: Support for up to 8 languages

Flexible Control: Up to 32 independently controlled zones

Reliable: UL864 certified for emergency voice systems

Integration: RS-485 and optional Ethernet communication

Standard Message Types
Fire Evacuation
"Fire alarm, please evacuate immediately"
Emergency Alert
"Emergency situation, follow instructions"

Voice Evacuation Benefits: Studies show that voice evacuation systems improve evacuation times by 30-50% compared to tone-only systems. Clear voice instructions reduce confusion, provide specific guidance, and can be tailored to different zones or building sections. The FW151's digital audio processing ensures message clarity even in noisy environments.

Message Planning: Effective voice evacuation requires careful message planning. Messages should be clear, concise, and provide specific instructions. Consider multiple languages based on occupant demographics. Test messages for intelligibility in various building areas with typical ambient noise levels.

Installation Guidelines
  • Install in clean, dry, temperature-controlled area
  • Maintain proper ventilation around unit
  • Follow NEC and NFPA 72 requirements
  • Provide adequate working clearance (36" minimum)
  • Use proper conduit and wiring methods
  • Ensure secure mounting to wall or rack
  • Verify audio circuit impedance matching

Installation Steps
  1. Select installation location per NFPA 72 requirements
  2. Mount unit securely to wall or 19" rack
  3. Route power wiring in approved conduit
  4. Connect audio lines to speaker circuits
  5. Connect communication lines (RS-485/Ethernet)
  6. Connect 24VDC power with proper polarity
  7. Program messages and zone assignments
  8. Configure language settings
  9. Test audio output at each speaker location
  10. Verify message clarity and intelligibility
  11. Document installation and programming
  12. Provide operator training

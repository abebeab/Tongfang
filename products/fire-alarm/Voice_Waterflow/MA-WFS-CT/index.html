Product Overview

The MA-WFS-CT T-Tap Flow Switch is a critical component in automatic fire sprinkler systems, designed to detect water flow when sprinklers activate. It provides immediate notification to the fire alarm system when water begins flowing through sprinkler pipes, enabling rapid emergency response and minimizing fire damage.

Flow Switch Importance: According to NFPA statistics, properly functioning sprinkler systems control 96% of fires in which they operate. Flow switches like the MA-WFS-CT ensure that fire departments are notified immediately when sprinklers activate, reducing response times and minimizing property damage. They are required by NFPA 13 and NFPA 72 for all automatic sprinkler systems.

Flow Switch Requirements: NFPA 13 requires that all automatic sprinkler systems have waterflow detection devices installed on each system riser. The MA-WFS-CT must be installed downstream of the control valve and upstream of the first sprinkler to ensure proper detection of water flow when sprinklers activate.

Installation Steps
  1. Verify sprinkler system is drained and safe to work on
  2. Select appropriate location on sprinkler pipe
  3. Drill and tap pipe for T-Tap connection
  4. Install flow switch body onto pipe fitting
  5. Adjust flow setting to system requirements
  6. Route wiring in approved conduit to switch location
  7. Connect wires to screw terminals
  8. Seal conduit entry with provided grommet
  9. Test switch operation using test valve
  10. Verify fire alarm system receives waterflow signal
  11. Document installation and testing results
  12. Label flow switch with appropriate identification

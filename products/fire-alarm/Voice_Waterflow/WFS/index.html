Product Overview

The WFS Waterflow Switch is an essential component in automatic fire sprinkler systems, designed to detect water movement when sprinklers activate. This critical safety device provides immediate notification to building occupants and emergency responders, significantly reducing response time and minimizing fire damage through early detection.

Flow Detection

Accurate detection of water flow in sprinkler systems.

Instant Alert

Immediate notification to fire alarm systems.

Adjustable

Field adjustable for different flow requirements.

Key Characteristics

Flow Detection: Paddle-type mechanism for reliable sensing

Durable Construction: Corrosion-resistant aluminum housing

Adjustable: Field adjustable flow settings (10-400 GPM)

Flexible Configuration: Normally open or normally closed operation

Electrical Rating: 5A @ 30VDC heavy-duty contacts

Pressure Rating: Up to 250 PSI operation

Critical Safety Component: Waterflow switches are required by NFPA 13 for all automatic sprinkler systems. Statistics show that properly functioning sprinkler systems with reliable waterflow detection control or extinguish 96% of fires where they operate. The WFS provides this critical early warning that can mean the difference between minor damage and catastrophic loss.

Code Compliance: NFPA 13 requires waterflow detection devices on all automatic sprinkler system risers. The WFS must be installed downstream of the control valve and upstream of the system's first sprinkler to ensure proper detection. Regular testing and maintenance are required by NFPA 25 to ensure continued reliability.

Installation Steps
  1. Verify sprinkler system is drained and safe to work on
  2. Select appropriate location on sprinkler pipe
  3. Cut pipe and install flow switch using provided fittings
  4. Adjust flow setting according to system requirements
  5. Route wiring in approved conduit to switch location
  6. Connect wires to heavy-duty screw terminals
  7. Seal conduit entry with provided waterproof grommet
  8. Test switch operation using test valve
  9. Verify fire alarm system receives waterflow signal
  10. Complete installation documentation and testing records
  11. Label flow switch with appropriate identification
  12. Provide maintenance and testing instructions to owner
